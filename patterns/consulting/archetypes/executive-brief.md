# Executive Brief

A dense but readable leadership memo: executive summaries, tight hierarchy, and
section dividers. For updates that respect a busy reader's time.

**Best for:** leadership updates, macroeconomic briefings, board memos.
**Visual language:** a blue or neutral corporate accent, dense-but-readable memo
slides, prominent executive summaries, clean section dividers. Charts and bullets
may coexist, but the hierarchy must be strict.

## Rules

- Lead with an executive summary; let the reader stop after it and still get it.
- Strict visual hierarchy — the eye always knows what is primary.
- Memo-style slides are allowed (more text than a recommendation deck), but every
  slide still has one message and an action title.
- Section dividers to segment a longer briefing.

## Pattern emphasis

C02 executive summary · C05 action-title evidence · C06 single chart · C10 KPI
row · C15 option comparison · C25 risk & mitigation · C30 recommendation ·
C33 assumptions.

## Note

A neutral archetype, inspired by the *grammar* of public executive briefing decks.
It copies no firm's template, palette, logo, or wording.
